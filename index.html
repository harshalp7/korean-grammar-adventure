Let's Master Korean Grammar!

1. -(으)면서 (While doing...)

This grammar point is used when the same person performs two actions at the same time. It's the perfect way to talk about multitasking!

사용법 (How to Use)

  • Verb stem ends in a vowel or ㄹ: -면서 (e.g., 가다 → 가면서, 놀다 → 놀면서)
  • Verb stem ends in a consonant: -으면서 (e.g., 먹다 → 먹으면서)
🎵

저는 음악을 들으면서 공부해요. (I study while listening to music.)

☕

커피를 마시면서 신문을 읽어요. (I read the newspaper while drinking coffee.)

📞

친구랑 이야기하면서 걸었어요. (I walked while talking with my friend.)

퀴즈 타임! (Quiz Time!) 🧠

Which sentence correctly says "I cook while watching YouTube"?

A) 유튜브를 보면서 요리해요.
B) 유튜브를 보고 요리해요.
정답! (Correct!) 🎉 "보면서" perfectly captures doing both actions at the same time. 잘했어요! (Well done!)
아니에요. (Not quite!) "보고" means "watch and then...", implying you first watch YouTube, then you cook. The actions are sequential, not simultaneous.

2. -기로 하다 (Decided to...)

This pattern expresses a decision, resolution, or promise. It's used to talk about future plans that you have firmly decided on.

사용법 (How to Use)

It's super easy! Just attach -기로 하다 to the verb stem. It doesn't matter if it ends in a vowel or consonant. (e.g., 가다 → 가기로 하다, 먹다 → 먹기로 하다)

🚭

새해부터 담배를 끊기로 했어요. (I decided to quit smoking from the new year.)

📚

우리는 주말에 도서관에서 공부하기로 했어요. (We decided to study at the library on the weekend.)

✈️

다음 휴가 때 제주도에 가기로 했어요. (I decided to go to Jeju Island for the next vacation.)

퀴즈 타임! (Quiz Time!) 🧠

How do you say "I decided to learn Korean"?

A) 한국어를 배울 거예요.
B) 한국어를 배우기로 했어요.
아깝다! (So close!) This means "I will learn Korean." It's a simple statement about the future, but it doesn't carry the strong feeling of a *decision* having been made.
완벽해요! (Perfect!) 👍 This emphasizes that you made a firm decision or resolution to learn Korean.

3. -아/어 보이다 (You look...)

Use this pattern with adjectives to describe the appearance or give an impression of someone or something. It's based on what you see and is like saying "looks," "seems," or "appears" in English.

사용법 (How to Use)

  • Adj. stem's last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ: -아 보이다 (e.g., 좋다 → 좋아 보이다)
  • Adj. stem's last vowel is anything else: -어 보이다 (e.g., 맛있다 → 맛있어 보이다)
  • Adjectives ending in 하다: -해 보이다 (e.g., 피곤하다 → 피곤해 보이다)
😊

오늘 행복해 보여요. 무슨 좋은 일 있어요? (You look happy today. Did something good happen?)

🧥

그 코트는 따뜻해 보여요. (That coat looks warm.)

🍰

이 케이크는 정말 맛있어 보여요! (This cake looks really delicious!)

퀴즈 타임! (Quiz Time!) 🧠

You see a new bag that looks expensive. What do you say?

A) 이 가방은 비싸 보여요.
B) 이 가방은 비싸요.
대박! (Awesome!) 💯 You're judging by its appearance, so "비싸 보여요" (It looks expensive) is the perfect expression.
조금만 더! (A little more!) "비싸요" is a factual statement ("It is expensive"). Since you're just guessing based on how it looks, "비싸 보여요" is more natural.
